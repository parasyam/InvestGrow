How ppf works

A Public Provident Fund (PPF) account allows individuals to make small savings while earning a fixed interest rate, with the interest being tax-free. Contributions can be made annually, and the account has a lock-in period of 15 years, with partial withdrawals allowed after the 7th year. The principal amount, interest earned, and maturity proceeds are all exempt from tax

pros
* Tax Benefits (upto 1.5 L.p.a)
* Low Risk
* Guaranteed Returns

cons

* Lower Returns
* Limited Contributions
* Lock-In Period
